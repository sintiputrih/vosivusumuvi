How Did Drew Carey Make His Multi-Million Dollar Net Worth?

Born and raised in Cleveland, Ohio, as the youngest of three children, Drew Carey grew up with a knack for music. As a youngster, he played the trumpet in his school's marching band. However, he appeared to switch interests when he joined the United States Marine Corps Reserve in 1980. After serving for five years, Carey channeled a comedy career performing on prestigious shows like "Late Night With David Letterman" and "The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson."

By 1994, the comic star launched his stand-up career, further catapulting his Hollywood career.

Drew Carey aced supporting roles in TV shows like "The Good Life," and soon enough, he debuted his eponymous comedy show, "The Drew Carey Show." Between 2001 and 2004, the star earned $750,000 per episode. The show spanned 233 episodes for nine seasons. For the last three seasons, Carey earned an estimated $59 million.

The former US Marine moved to TV hosting in 2007, debuting on the CBS-aired game show "Power of 10." It would not be long before he replaced Bob Barker on "The Price Is Right," where he made some of his peak career earnings. It is safe to say Drew Carey has continued to be one of the highest-paid TV hosts.
