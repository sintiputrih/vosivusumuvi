Early Life & Biography

Richard Madden was born in Elderslie, Renfrewshire, on 18 June 1986 to his parents Pat and Richard. His father was a firefighter, and his mother was a primary school teacher. He has two siblings with whom he grew up in the city of Glasgow. As a child, Madden was very shy; therefore, he joined PACE Youth Theatre to overcome his shyness.

While studying acting at the theatre, he was soon cast as a child artist in the movie adaptation of the novel Complicity in 2000. He was also seen in the children’s television series, Barmy Aunt Boomerang.

He went to the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland, where he graduated in the year 2007. While he was in college, he worked with the Glasgow Repertory Company and played the character of Tom Fool at the Citizens’ Theatre.

Career

Richard Madden started his career while he was still in college. In his final year, he was cast in the production of Romeo and Juliet at the prestigious Globe Theatre in the UK. In 2008, he was seen in the series Noughts & Crosses as Callum McGregor. He then played the lead role in the National Theatre of Scotland’s production of Be Near Me.

In 2009, Madden starred as Dean McKenzie in the comedy-drama series Hope Springs which aired on the BBC network. The following year, he played the role of Ripley in the 2010 thriller-drama movie Chatroom. He was also seen as Kirk Brandon in the movie Worried About The Boy, which was released in 2010.

Madden became a household name when he starred as Robb Stark in the popular fantasy drama television series Game of Thrones. His performance received critical acclaim and was also appreciated by the audience. At the same time, he also starred in the comedy-drama series Sirens and BBC drama series Birdsong.

In 2015, he played the role of a prince in the Disney movie Cinderella which received positive reviews. Along with his Cinderella co-star Lily James, Madden was seen in the play Romeo and Juliet in which he played Romeo. In the Italian-British television drama series, Medici, he portrayed the character of Cosimo de’ Medici. In the same year, he played the role of pickpocket Michael Mason in the action movie Bastille Day.

Awards & Achievements

Richard Madden has won several awards for his brilliant performances in movies and television shows. For his role as Sergeant David Budd, a war veteran in the thriller series Bodyguard, he was given the Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Television Series Drama. The television series also became the most-watched television drama in the UK.

The series was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award in the category of Outstanding Drama Series.

In 2009, Madden won the Most Stylish Male at the Scottish Style Awards. The following year, he was included in the list of Esquire’s Hottest Stars. In 2011, he was named one of Screen International’s Stars of Tomorrow.

In 2019, he won the GQ Men of the Year Awards and was also named one of the 100 most influential people in the world by Time magazine.
