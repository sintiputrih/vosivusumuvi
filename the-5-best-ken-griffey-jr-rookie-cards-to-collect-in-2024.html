The 5 best Ken Griffey Jr. Rookie Cards to Collect in 2024

August 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Ken Griffey Jr., a renowned baseball legend, has left an indelible mark on the sport, and his rookie cards are highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts.

In 2024, these cards continue to hold substantial value in the market, making them prized possessions for those looking to enhance their collections.

1. 1989 Bowman Tiffany #220 Ken Griffey Jr. Rookie Card

The 1989 Bowman Tiffany #220 Ken Griffey Jr. rookie card is a coveted possession due to its limited print run of about 6,000 sets, premium white cardstock, glossy finish, and clean white back.

This card has a rich history, being a cornerstone of Bowman's baseball card resurgence in 1989, which adds to its allure.

While the design of this card might be considered lackluster, its relative scarcity propels high sales.

The five-year average on the highest grade is between $9,000 and $10,000, making it a valuable addition to any collector's portfolio.

2. 1989 Donruss The Rookies Ken Griffey Jr. RC #3

The 1989 Donruss The Rookies Ken Griffey Jr. RC #3 holds significant value with a sale price of $187.

This card is a desirable item for collectors seeking a piece of baseball history.

With a relatively low supply and high demand, this card continues to be a valuable asset in the world of sports memorabilia. Its historical significance and rarity make it an essential addition to any collection.

3. 1989 Fleer Glossy Ken Griffey Jr. RC #548

The 1989 Fleer Glossy Ken Griffey Jr. RC #548 holds significant value, as evidenced by its sale price of $4,920.

This card is believed to have a very limited print run of only 3,000 cards, contributing to its allure.

Despite the design being considered bland with a gray striped background and an over-exposed photo of Griffey holding a bat, the scarcity of this card is the driving force behind its value.

With a five-year average of $2,750, the Fleer Glossy #548 is a card that collectors will want to keep an eye on.

4. 1989 Score Young Superstars Series II #18

The 1989 Score Young Superstars Series II #18 features a unique alternating thick and thin border design and frame along the bottom of the card.

Score printed the Young Superstars set as a small series run that only included 42 cards.

"The Kid" in mid-swing is the highlight of Series II. With one of the better Griffey action shots, this card has been running around $120 on a five-year average, making it a valuable addition to any personal collection for the action shot alone.

5. 1989 Topps Traded Tiffany #41T

The 1989 Topps Traded Tiffany #41T, with a price range of $350-375 for the highest grades, is a great addition to any Griffey Rookie collection.

This card boasts a unique design and scarcity compared to every other Griffey rookie, making it a card that few collectors have.

Its limited run and distinct features contribute to its desirability and value in the market, making it a must-have for collectors.

To wrap things up

These top five Ken Griffey Jr. rookie cards showcase his enduring legacy in the world of baseball and are highly sought after by collectors due to their historical significance, rarity, and unique features.

Whether for investment or personal collection, these cards continue to hold substantial value in the market, symbolizing the nostalgia and admiration for the player while serving as valuable assets in the world of sports memorabilia.

These cards not only represent the legacy of Ken Griffey Jr. but also stand as prized possessions for those passionate about the sport and the hobby of card collecting.
