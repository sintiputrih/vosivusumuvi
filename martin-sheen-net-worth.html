Martin Sheen | Films and TV Shows & Books

Films and TV Shows

Martin Sheen started a theatre company with other actors hoping to gain attention.

He made an appearance on the TV sci-fi series The Outer Limits in 1963.

With that and a series of other smaller gigs, he had gained a co-starring role in the Broadway play The Subject was Roses in 1964.

He reprised his role in 1968 in a film of the same name garnering a Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actor.

Similarly, he made a guest appearances in Hawaii Five-O, Mod Squad, My Three Sons, and numerous others.

Sheen co-starred in the Emmy-winning TV film That Certain Summer in 1972.

It was the first TV film in American history to portray homosexuality in a positive light which was controversial for the time.

Even though he had appeared in numerous TV series and films before Sheen’s major breakthrough came in the year 1979 with Francis Ford Coppola’s Apocalypse Now.

Then came the series of blockbusters, including, The West Wing, The Departed, Grace, and Frankie.

Martin Sheen has more than 300 acting credits to his name as of this writing, and his films have grossed over $2.7 billion at the global box office as a supporting actor.

In addition to these, Sheen has also narrated several films, including Seeking a Friend for the End of the World, Catch Me If You Can, Trash, Vessel, Rules Don’t Apply, and Selma.
